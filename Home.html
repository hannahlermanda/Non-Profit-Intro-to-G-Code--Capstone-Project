What We Do

Give South Central children and their families a safe haven to grow 💞 🏠


"I really want to start a place for all the children to go to - to be safe, to feel safe."

-Debrah Constance, Founder


Since 1993, A Place Called Home has made it its mission to provide the canvas for South Central youth to mold their futures with purpose. APCH strives to provide resources and opportunities for at-risk young people and their families, so that they are empowered to take control of their lives. Since our founding and our original 12 members, we have grown and served over 20,000 youths and their families in the South Central area. We are determined to continue helping the future thrive through the investment in our children and their families.

Who We Serve

The youth of South Central Los Angeles, their families, and the community 💫


APCH fosters us even though we come from a tough neighborhood. Anything is possible with hard work, mentorship, and community.

-Cindy Diaz, APCH Alumni

A Place Called Home serves youth ages 8-24 and their family by providing resources that contribute to their holistic growth and development. Residents of the community we serve are historically under-resourced and face adverse challenges, such as high unemployment and crime rates and low graduation rates and income. APCH gives developing youth a safe place to come to provide resources and a supportive community that encourages them to foster their dreams and talents to fruition for lifelong success and happiness.

Our Programs

"We give them a space to think, a space to create, and a space to challenge their own thoughts, and at the very least, tools to navigate the world."

- Dr. Lucretia Taylor, Program Director (Teen & Young Adult Services) 🌎

Our programs include free year-round services such as:

Education Services

We offer one-on-one tutoring (in English, math, science, history, and cultural identity) and literacy training for our members. These services are made possible by our hired instructors and lovely volunteers who take the time to empower, support, and teach our youth from their elementary years all the way through college. Academic enrichment activities, such as book and poetry clubs, are also offered.

Art, Music, Dance, and other Creative Expressions Program

In our creative arts program, we encourage our members's social and emotional growth through the use of their imaginations and talents in an art program of their choice. We have class in fine arts (painting, drawing, etc.), dance, digital arts (including video), photography, music, music production and technology, poetry, theater, and fashion design. APCH has invested in state of the art equipment and staff and created partnerships in order to offer the best tools for our youth to have a positive outlet for their artistic expressions and start their potential professional careers.

Community Engagement and Outreach

We believe that a community's success and child's growth go hand-in-hand. At APCH, we focus on supporting the community by providing basic necessities, career development, classes, and other resources to uplift and develop our people. We also teach our youth the importance of giving back to the community by providing them with opportunities to volunteer and/or assist other members through APCH.

Health and Nutrition

APCH takes a holistic approach to our members's and community's well-being. In order to thrive, our youth need their basic necessities met and be given the tools to manage whatever problems they may come across in their lives. We offer several services and classes to ensure APCH members are healthy physically, mentally, and spirtually. This includes:

  • Mental Health Services/Sessions
  • Athletics
  • Nutrition and Urban Agriculture

Bridge to the Future Programs

For our teen and older members, we offer a variety of programs that offer them the keys to open the doors to their futures. These programs give teens the tools to achieve a higher education, be ready for employment, manage their finances, and gain bonds with one another during a key time in their development. The programs offered are:

  • College-Bound/APCH Shaheen Scholarship Program
  • Professional Readiness and Employment Pathways (PREP)
  • Financial Education and Life Skills
  • GirlPower
  • Journey

Rise at APCH

Da Vinci Rise at APCH is a high school for the most at-risk youth in the community, including those in the foster care system, who have been incarcerated, who struggle with housing, that have had their academic journies disrupted for whatever reason. At this school, youth can recover credits, obtain a high school diploma, prepare for college, participate in internships, receive counseling, and get the support they need to build the foundations for their future.
